Hi, my name's Jonathan! I'm a Canadian composer, multi-instrumentalist producer, and classically trained jazz musician of Cantonese and Scottish heritage. Drawing from a diverse background of creative influence, my work spans traditional orchestration, cross-cultural composition, and modern sound design.

My musical journey began from the age of five, training in classical piano within the Royal Conservatory of Music. During my teenage years, however, my impatience with the rigidity of the classical canon led me to stumble upon the improvisatory world of jazz; the works of Hiromi Uehara, Oscar Peterson, and Chick Corea ultimately inspired me to explore music beyond the conservatory and further beyond western culture. From then on, improvisation and multiculturalism became a focal point of my musical journey. I began with jazz piano before inexplicably transitioning to finger-style and flamenco guitar, then began delving into my Chinese roots learning the erhu until shifting towards hip-hop and digital audio production. My aspiration was suddenly to become a musical polyglot, a development especially pertinent in my choice to become a composer.

As a composer, my journey began in Vancouver as part of the UBC game development club. There, I was given the opportunity to score a VR game, and was so riveted by the process that I promptly converted my major to music composition. I eventually settled at the University of Lethbridge, where I not only carried out my formal studies but also continued fuelling my multi-instrumentalist addiction and passion for orchestration by renting every instrument I could find. As a student, I continued composing for games and animations while training in concert music under the mentorship of Dr. Arlan N. Schultz. At the end of March 2024, after an unforgettable semester of collaboration with fellow musicians in my graduation recital, I concluded my studies and began my journey into freelance composing and audio production. My work can be heard here.
