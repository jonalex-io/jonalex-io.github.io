Hi, my name's Jonathan! I'm a Canadian composer, multi-instrumentalist producer, and classically trained jazz musician of Cantonese and Scottish heritage. Drawing from a diverse background of creative influence, my work spans traditional orchestration, cross-cultural composition, and modern sound design.

My musical journey began from the age of five, training in classical piano under the Royal Conservatory of Music. While I excelled into my teenage years, however, my eventual impatience with the rigidity of the classical canon led me to stumble upon the improvisatory world of jazz, where the works of Hiromi Uehara, Oscar Peterson, and Chick Corea inspired my exploration beyond the conservatory and further beyond western culture. From then on, improvisation and multiculturalism became a focal point of my musical journeyâ€”â€”from jazz piano, I inexplicably transitioned to flamenco guitar, then began exploring my Chinese roots learning the erhu, then shifted yet again into hip-hop and digital audio production. My aspiration was suddenly to become a musical polyglot, a development especially pertinent in my choice to become a composer.

As a composer, my journey began in UBC Vancouverâ€™s game development club. There, I was given the opportunity to score a VR game, and was so riveted by the process that I promptly converted my major to music composition. I eventually settled at the University of Lethbridge, where I not only carried out my formal studies but also continued fuelling my multi-instrumentalist addiction and passion for orchestration by renting every instrument I could find. As a student, I continued composing for games and animations while training in concert music under the mentorship of Dr. Arlan N. Schultz. At the end of March 2024, after an unforgettable semester of collaboration with fellow musicians in my graduation recital, I concluded my studies and began my journey into freelance composing and audio production. My work can be heard here.
